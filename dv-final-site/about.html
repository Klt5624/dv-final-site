About this project

This website is an awareness and education project about domestic violence — created to be bold, direct, and empowering. Domestic violence is not limited to one gender, age, income level, or background. It can happen to anyone.

What “abuse” can look like

Physical harm is only one part. Many survivors describe control, fear, isolation, and constant monitoring long before any visible injury.

What support can look like

Support is listening, believing, and helping someone explore options safely — without pressure or shame.

What safety can look like

Safety includes boundaries, trusted relationships, and access to resources — and it always includes choice.

Myth: “If it were really bad, they would just leave.”

Leaving can be the most dangerous time. Survivors may face financial control, threats, isolation, childcare barriers, fear of retaliation, or lack of safe housing. Support + planning can reduce risk.

Myth: “Abuse is only physical.”

Abuse can include emotional manipulation, stalking, financial control, intimidation, sexual coercion, and digital surveillance. The goal is power and control.
