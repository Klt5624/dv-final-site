Resources & support

The best resource is the one you can safely access. If you are in immediate danger, call local emergency services. For non-emergency help, consider local shelters, advocacy centers, or a trusted professional.

Resource directory (table)

This table is included to meet the project’s table requirement. Please verify local contacts in your area.

Type What they help with How to find Best time
Emergency services Immediate danger, urgent protection Call your local emergency number (e.g., 911 in the U.S.) Right now
DV hotline / advocacy Safety planning, shelter referrals, confidential support Search: “domestic violence hotline” + your city/state Anytime (many are 24/7)
Local shelter Safe housing, case management, support groups Search: “DV shelter near me” or ask an advocacy hotline When a safe exit plan is needed
Legal aid Protective orders, custody guidance, legal resources Search: “legal aid” + your county After immediate safety is addressed
Counseling / therapy Trauma support, healing, coping tools Search: “trauma-informed therapist” + your area Ongoing recovery

What you can do as a friend

  • Listen and believe them.
  • Ask: “What do you need right now?”
  • Offer choices and support their pace.
  • Help them document incidents safely if they want to.
  • Don’t confront the abuser — focus on survivor safety.
